Students Against Gun Violence

It all started when our group of dedicated students from Long Island came together to organize March For Our Lives Farmingdale with Congressman Suozzi. In the wake of the tragic shooting in Parkland, thousands of students felt frustrated with a lack of progress toward common sense gun laws, and like many movements of the past and present, sought to make a change. Many of us had organized protests, walk-outs, and displays of support for MSD in our high schools prior to meeting, and we all shared the common goal of making our country a safer place to live.

Josh

My name is Josh, I am an incoming Freshman at NYU. I became interested in politics during my AP American History class. At that time, mass shootings were becoming more and more common. We live in a society where mass shootings have become the norm. How can we have laws that allow people to easily acquire an automatic weapon? Because of the terrible gun laws in this country, so many children will never be able to graduate college, get married or have children of their own. The only way to keep the legacy of these kids alive is to go out and vote. We need to vote out anyone who cares more about money than people’s lives.

Lily

My name is Lily, and I am a rising sophomore in North Shore High School. Throughout the duration of my education I have been exposed to the relentless worrying of my parents, peers, and teachers, as well as intensifying my own anxiety regarding the safety of my school. No one should ever have to walk into school not knowing how their day will end because of the unnecessary and constant instances of gun violence occurring in our country. I stand with gun violence prevention because no child, including myself, should feel threatened by the inappropriate use of firearms-especially when we are in an environment that should focus solely on our education. With all that is going on in our country, it is important that we exercise our opinions and beliefs in the most American way possible- voting. Using your voice and putting the right people in office goes a long way in solving complex issues, such as gun violence prevention.

Andrew

I’m Andrew and I’m going to be starting at the University of Michigan this fall. After the events at Parkland earlier this year, I was mobilized to take action to ensure that no student would have to go to school every day in fear of their lives. At Syosset High School, I helped organize our school’s memorial and walkout with over a thousand students participating. Subsequently, I was interviewed by NBC 4 about work I have done with Nassau County DA Madeline Singas’ school safety task force, of which I was one of two student representatives. I always vote because I believe that voting enables us, as young proud citizens, to have a tangible, meaningful impact on how our country is governed.

Gia

I'm Gia Yetikyel and I’m a rising freshman at Northwestern University. There was an actual lockdown at my high school last year, and since then, I tend to flinch at loud noises or the intercom speaker going off. It’s exhausting having to constantly figure out ways to escape a room just in case I’m put in a situation like that again. I support gun control because I don’t want my little brother having to experience the same fear I do now. It’s utterly critical that we commit to creating change through our actions, like voting. I vote in hopes that this epidemic of gun violence will be put to a stop.

Madeleine

I’m Madeleine and I am a senior at Northport High School. I have always been interested in politics but I became particularly involved with the issue of gun violence after the Marjory Stoneman Douglas shooting, because I realized that the issue could directly affect not only me but my siblings in elementary and middle school. I hope that this rally helps to inspire students to get involved in politics and to use their vote in November in order to make their voices heard.
